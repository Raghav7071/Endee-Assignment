Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) was launched on 1 May 2016 to provide free LPG connections to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households.
The scheme aims to safeguard the health of women and children by providing clean cooking fuel and reducing indoor air pollution.
Under PMUY, eligible families receive a free LPG connection along with a financial support of Rs 1600 for each LPG connection.
The scheme initially targeted providing 5 crore LPG connections but the target was later revised to 8 crore connections.
As of 2024, over 9.5 crore free LPG connections have been released under PMUY across India.
The scheme has significantly reduced the dependence on traditional cooking fuels like firewood, coal, and cow dung cakes.
PMUY 2.0 was launched in August 2021 with additional benefits including a free first refill and a stove to all Ujjwala beneficiaries.
The scheme has played a key role in improving air quality in rural households and reducing respiratory diseases among women.
