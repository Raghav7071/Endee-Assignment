Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) is a scheme launched in April 2015 to provide loans up to Rs 10 lakh to non-corporate, non-farm small and micro enterprises.
MUDRA stands for Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency Limited.
The scheme offers three categories of loans: Shishu (up to Rs 50,000), Kishore (Rs 50,001 to Rs 5 lakh), and Tarun (Rs 5 lakh to Rs 10 lakh).
No collateral is required for loans under the MUDRA scheme making it accessible to first-time entrepreneurs.
The loans are available through banks, NBFCs, MFIs, and other lending institutions.
MUDRA loans support activities in manufacturing, trading, service sector, and agriculture-allied activities.
Over 40 crore loans have been sanctioned under PMMY since its inception, promoting self-employment and entrepreneurship.
Women entrepreneurs form a significant portion of MUDRA loan beneficiaries, supporting women empowerment.
