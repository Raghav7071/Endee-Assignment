Skill India Mission was launched on 15 July 2015 with the aim of training over 40 crore people in India in different skills by 2022.
The mission is implemented by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE).
Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) is the flagship scheme under Skill India that provides free short-term skill training and certification.
Under PMKVY, training is provided through registered training centres across the country in over 300 job roles across various sectors.
Trainees who successfully complete the assessment receive a government-recognized certificate and placement assistance.
The National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) is the implementing agency that works with training partners to deliver skill courses.
Skill India also supports Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to certify the existing skills of informal sector workers.
The Jan Shikshan Sansthan programme under Skill India provides vocational training to non-literate and school dropout populations.
Over 1.5 crore candidates have been trained under PMKVY since its launch across multiple phases.
